Who am I?

As a developer, I’ve faced many different challenges throughout my career. Originally, I’d never decided on being a computer scientist, rather a lawyer. From a young age, I was captivated by computers and everything that they did. Yet, I was often left with a burning question in the back of my head: “How do they do it?”.

All of this remained an enigma until I took my first computer science course in 2014. Creating websites, styling them, adding interactive items, and even learning javascript to some extent captivated my interest. It wasn’t until I opened up IDLE and started using Python that I’d truly begun my journey.

Soon, the basis of python wasn’t enough for me, so I started studying libraries. Tkinter was an obvious first choice, and Pygame not long after. Over the next seven years, I started studying everything, from Unity and the C family, to Java and even some basic Assembly programming.

Inevitably, after learning all I could about the fundamentals of computer science and the inner workings of a computer, I simplified my interests. Instead, returning to where I came from, web development.

Since then, I’ve been working simultaneously to bridge the gap between server management and web development in my skillset, namely studying the WAMP architecture (Windows, Apache, MySQL, PHP).

While I have aspirations of gaining my masters in Computer Science in the future, short-term, I’m looking for employment as an intern to learn more about both web development and software development.
